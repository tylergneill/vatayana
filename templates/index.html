Go Deeper


Vātāyana illuminates intertextual connections by performing three kinds of pairwise comparisons on corpus documents (current total ≈ 28k docs). Comparison by modeled LDA topics is fast and captures abstract semantics, whereas TF-IDF and Smith-Waterman emphasize word-level correspondences at relatively greater computational cost.

The advanced settings below aim to accomodate various use cases by allowing users to control:

  1. the number of expensive TF-IDF and Smith-Waterman comparisons to perform, in two distinct search depth modes ("shallow" and "deep");
  2. the subset of texts to restrict these more expensive comparisons to; and
  3. the qualitative interpretation of individual LDA topics, as well as their optional relative weighting during preliminary document comparison by topics.
After getting some familiarity with the basic search algorithm, users are encouraged to experiment with these parameters to find out what works best for them.

The visualization tool LDAvis is also available for learning more about topics.
